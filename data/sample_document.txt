GLOBAL FINANCIAL SERVICES ADVISORY FRAMEWORK (GFSAF) 
Regulatory Document Version 3.2 
Effective: January 1, 2096 
Issued by: International Financial Oversight Commission (IFOC) 
TOPIC 1: DEFINITIONS AND SCOPE 
1.1 Purpose 
This Framework establishes comprehensive standards for financial advisory services, investment management, and fiduciary obligations across participating jurisdictions. 
1.2 Definitions 
For purposes of this Framework: 
(a) "Financial Institution" means any entity engaged in providing financial advisory 
services, investment management, or fiduciary services with assets under management exceeding $10 million or serving more than 50 clients annually. 
(b) "Qualified Advisor" means an individual who: 
(i) Holds appropriate licensure in their jurisdiction 
(ii) Has completed mandatory training requirements per Topic 8 
(iii) Maintains professional liability insurance per Topic 12 
(iv) Has no disqualifying violations per Topic 22 
(c) "Client" means any natural person, corporation, trust, or entity receiving financial advisory services. 
(d) "Zone-C Advisors" means financial advisors who maintain substantial operations in Zone-C and meet the enhanced qualification requirements specified herein. 
1.3 Applicability 
This Framework applies to all Financial Institutions and Qualified Advisors operating 
within participating jurisdictions, including Zone-A, Zone-B, and Zone-C territories. 
TOPIC 2: REGISTRATION AND LICENSING REQUIREMENTS 
2.1 Initial Registration 
All Financial Institutions must register with their primary jurisdictional authority within 
60 days of commencing operations. 
2.2 Annual Renewal 
Registration must be renewed annually by December 31st, accompanied by: 
(a) Updated Form ADV or equivalent disclosure 
(b) Proof of insurance per Topic 12 
(c) Compliance certification per Topic 15 
(d) Fee payment per Schedule A 
2.3 Multi-Jurisdictional Operations 
Financial Institutions operating across multiple zones must: 
(a) Register in each zone where clients are domiciled 
(b) Designate a Zone-C Advisor for clients in Zone-C 
(c) Comply with the most stringent requirements among applicable jurisdictions 
TOPIC 3: FIDUCIARY OBLIGATIONS 
3.1 Duty of Care 
Financial Institutions shall exercise the care, skill, prudence, and diligence that a 
prudent advisor would exercise in similar circumstances, as detailed in Topic 22. 
3.2 Duty of Loyalty 
Advisors must act in the best interests of clients and disclose all material conflicts of 
interest within 10 business days of discovery. 
3.3 Disclosure Requirements 
All material facts affecting advisory relationships must be disclosed, including: 
(a) Compensation structures 
(b) Affiliated entities 
(c) Custody arrangements 
(d) Beneficial ownership per Topic 31 
TOPIC 4: CLIENT QUALIFICATION AND SUITABILITY 
4.1 Know Your Client (KYC) 
Before accepting any client, Financial Institutions must: 
(a) Verify client identity per Topic 6 
(b) Assess financial sophistication 
(c) Determine investment objectives 
(d) Evaluate risk tolerance 
(e) Document all findings unless Topic 15 exemption applies 
4.2 Ongoing Monitoring 
Client profiles must be updated: 
(a) Annually at minimum 
(b) Upon material change in circumstances 
(c) Before recommending significant portfolio changes 
4.3 Restricted Clients 
Financial Institutions may not accept clients who: 
(a) Are subject to sanctions per Topic 19 
(b) Cannot satisfy enhanced due diligence per Topic 30 
(c) Reside in non-participating jurisdictions unless authorized under Topic 27 
TOPIC 5: ADVISORY SERVICES STANDARDS 
5.1 Investment Recommendations 
All investment recommendations must: 
(a) Be suitable for the client per Topic 4.2 
(b) Consider diversification requirements 
(c) Account for tax implications 
(d) Be documented in writing 
5.1.a High-Risk Investments 
For high-risk investments (as defined in Topic 22), additional disclosure requirements apply per Topic 18. 
5.2 Portfolio Management 
Discretionary portfolio management requires: 
(a) Written authorization from client 
(b) Investment Policy Statement (IPS) 
(c) Quarterly performance reporting 
(d) Annual compliance review per Topic 15 
TOPIC 6: ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML) COMPLIANCE 
6.1 Customer Identification Program (CIP) 
Financial Institutions must implement robust CIP procedures including: 
(a) Government-issued identification verification 
(b) Address verification 
(c) Beneficial ownership identification per Topic 31 
(d) Enhanced due diligence for Zone-C operations 
6.2 Suspicious Activity Reporting 
Suspicious activities must be reported within: 
(a) 24 hours for terrorism-related activities 
(b) 30 days for other suspicious activities 
(c) Per procedures established under Topic 9 
6.3 Recordkeeping 
AML records must be retained for minimum 7 years unless longer period required by Topic 30. 
TOPIC 7: COMPENSATION AND FEE DISCLOSURE 
7.1 Fee Structures 
Financial Institutions may charge: 
(a) Assets-under-management (AUM) fees not exceeding 2% annually 
(b) Hourly fees for consultation 
(c) Performance-based fees only for Qualified Clients per Topic 13 
(d) Transaction-based compensation subject to disclosure 
7.2 Prohibition on Excessive Fees 
Fees must be reasonable and competitive with industry standards. Excessive fees constitute a violation subject to penalties per Topic 25. 
7.3 Third-Party Compensation 
All third-party compensation must be disclosed, including: 
(a) Referral fees 
(b) Revenue sharing arrangements 
(c) Soft dollar arrangements 
(d) Payments from Zone-C Advisors 
TOPIC 8: PROFESSIONAL QUALIFICATIONS AND TRAINING 
8.1 Minimum Qualifications 
Qualified Advisors must possess: 
(a) Bachelor's degree or equivalent 
(b) Relevant professional certification (CFP, CFA, or equivalent) 
(c) 3 years of industry experience 
(d) Clean regulatory record per Topic 22 
8.2 Continuing Education 
Annual continuing education requirements: 
(a) 20 hours of approved coursework 
(b) 5 hours of ethics training 
(c) 10 hours of technical training 
(d) Specialized training for Zone-C operations 
8.3 Examination Requirements 
Initial qualification requires passing: 
(a) General Knowledge Examination (minimum 75% score) 
(b) Ethics Examination (minimum 85% score) 
(c) Jurisdiction-specific examination where applicable 
TOPIC 9: AUDIT AND COMPLIANCE PROCEDURES 
9.1 Internal Controls 
Financial Institutions must maintain: 
(a) Written compliance policies and procedures 
(b) Chief Compliance Officer (CCO) designation 
(c) Annual compliance review 
(d) Internal audit function (for firms with AUM > $100 million) 
9.2 External Audits 
Annual financial statement audits required for: 
(a) Institutions with AUM exceeding $50 million 
(b) Institutions with custody of client assets 
(c) Unless exempt under Topic 15 
**NOTE: Audit not required if Topic 15 exemption applies** 
9.3 Regulatory Examinations 
Institutions must cooperate with regulatory examinations and provide: 
(a) Access to all books and records 
(b) Employee interviews 
(c) Electronic data in requested format 
(d) Responses within time frames specified by examiner 
TOPIC 10: MARKETING AND ADVERTISING STANDARDS 
10.1 Truthful Advertising 
All marketing materials must: 
(a) Be accurate and not misleading 
(b) Include required risk disclosures 
(c) Not promise specific returns 
(d) Comply with Topic 18 restrictions 
10.2 Performance Advertising 
When advertising performance: 
(a) Must include appropriate benchmarks 
(b) Time periods must be disclosed 
(c) Net-of-fees returns required 
(d) Past performance disclaimers mandatory 
10.3 Testimonials and Endorsements 
Use of client testimonials permitted only if: 
(a) Compensation disclosed if paid 
(b) Not cherry-picked or misleading 
(c) Representative of client experiences 
(d) Compliant with Topic 24 standards 
TOPIC 11: CUSTODY AND SAFEKEEPING OF CLIENT ASSETS 
11.1 Custody Requirements 
Financial Institutions maintaining custody must: 
(a) Use qualified custodian 
(b) Provide quarterly account statements 
(c) Undergo surprise audit annually 
(d) Maintain fidelity bond per Topic 12.f 
11.2 Prohibited Practices 
Financial Institutions shall not: 
(a) Commingle client and firm assets 
(b) Borrow from client accounts 
(c) Use client assets as collateral 
(d) Engage in unauthorized trading 
11.3 Account Statement Requirements 
Client statements must include: 
(a) All transactions during period 
(b) Beginning and ending values 
(c) Fees charged 
(d) Performance metrics per Topic 5 
TOPIC 12: INSURANCE AND BONDING REQUIREMENTS 
12.1 Professional Liability Insurance 
All Financial Institutions must maintain: 
(a) Errors and Omissions (E&O) insurance 
(b) Minimum coverage: $1 million per occurrence 
(c) Aggregate coverage: $2 million annually 
(d) Policy must cover all advisory activities 
12.2 Cyber Liability Insurance 
For institutions managing client data electronically: 
(a) Minimum $500,000 cyber liability coverage 
(b) Coverage for data breach notifications 
(c) Coverage for regulatory penalties 
12.3 Fidelity Bonds 
(This section references Topic 12.f below) 
12.4 Proof of Insurance 
Insurance certificates must be filed: 
(a) At initial registration 
(b) Annually at renewal 
(c) Within 15 days of policy changes 
(d) Per format specified in Appendix C 
12.5 Lapse of Coverage 
Failure to maintain required insurance constitutes: 
(a) Automatic suspension of registration 
(b) Prohibition on accepting new clients 
(c) Violation subject to penalties per Topic 25 
12.f Fidelity Bond Requirements 
Institutions with custody of client assets must maintain fidelity bonds covering: 
(a) All employees with access to client assets 
(b) Minimum $100,000 or 1% of AUM (whichever greater) 
(c) Maximum $500,000 unless higher amount required 
TOPIC 13: PERFORMANCE-BASED COMPENSATION 
13.1 Qualified Client Standard 
Performance-based fees permitted only for clients with: 
(a) Net worth exceeding $2.5 million, OR 
(b) AUM with advisor exceeding $1.5 million, OR 
(c) Qualified Purchaser status 
13.2 Fee Calculation Methods 
Acceptable performance fee structures: 
(a) Percentage of profits over high-water mark 
(b) Fulcrum fees (symmetric around benchmark) 
(c) Hurdle rate structures 
13.3 Disclosure Requirements 
Performance fee arrangements require: 
(a) Written agreement signed by client 
(b) Detailed fee calculation methodology 
(c) Hypothetical fee scenarios 
(d) Annual performance reports per Topic 5.2 
TOPIC 14: CONFLICTS OF INTEREST MANAGEMENT 
14.1 Identification 
Financial Institutions must identify conflicts including: 
(a) Personal trading 
(b) Affiliated entities 
(c) Referral arrangements 
(d) Proprietary product recommendations 
14.2 Disclosure 
Material conflicts must be disclosed: 
(a) In writing at account opening 
(b) In Form ADV Part 2A 
(c) Before the conflict arises 
(d) Annually thereafter 
14.3 Mitigation Procedures 
For conflicts that cannot be eliminated: 
(a) Implement information barriers 
(b) Obtain client consent 
(c) Employ independent oversight 
(d) Document mitigation measures 
TOPIC 15: COMPLIANCE EXEMPTIONS AND SAFE HARBORS 
15.1 Small Advisor Exemption 
Financial Institutions with AUM below $25 million may be exempt from: 
(a) Topic 9.2 external audit requirement 
(b) Topic 21 third-party verification 
(c) Certain recordkeeping requirements per Topic 30 
**IMPORTANT: Before Topic 15 exemption can be claimed, must complete audit per Topic 9** 
15.2 Limited Advisory Services Exemption 
Institutions providing only: 
(a) General financial education 
(b) Non-discretionary advice 
(c) Fewer than 15 clients annually 
May qualify for simplified compliance per Section 15.3 
15.3 Exemption Application Process 
To claim exemption: 
(a) File Form EX-15 with jurisdictional authority 
(b) Provide supporting documentation 
(c) Receive written confirmation 
(d) Renew exemption annually 
15.4 Exemption Limitations 
Exemptions under this Topic do not waive: 
(a) Anti-fraud provisions 
(b) Fiduciary duties per Topic 3 
(c) AML requirements per Topic 6 
(d) Client disclosure obligations 
TOPIC 16: CYBERSECURITY AND DATA PROTECTION 
16.1 Minimum Security Standards 
Financial Institutions must implement: 
(a) Multi-factor authentication 
(b) Encryption of sensitive data 
(c) Regular security assessments 
(d) Incident response plan 
16.2 Data Breach Notification 
In event of data breach: 
(a) Notify regulatory authority within 48 hours 
(b) Notify affected clients within 72 hours 
(c) Provide credit monitoring services 
(d) File incident report per Topic 28 
16.3 Third-Party Service Providers 
When using external service providers: 
(a) Conduct due diligence 
(b) Maintain written contracts 
(c) Monitor ongoing performance 
(d) Ensure provider compliance with Topic 16.1 
TOPIC 17: CROSS-BORDER SERVICES 
17.1 Multi-Jurisdictional Registration 
See Topic 2.3 for registration requirements across zones. 
17.2 Currency and Exchange Controls 
For clients with assets in multiple currencies: 
(a) Disclosure of exchange rate risks 
(b) Reporting in client's base currency 
(c) Compliance with capital controls 
(d) Enhanced monitoring for Zone-C transactions 
17.3 Tax Reporting 
Financial Institutions must: 
(a) Comply with FATCA where applicable 
(b) Report foreign account holdings per jurisdiction 
(c) Withhold taxes as required 
(d) Provide tax documentation to clients annually 
TOPIC 18: RESTRICTIONS ON HIGH-RISK ACTIVITIES 
18.1 Prohibited Investments 
Financial Institutions shall not recommend: 
(a) Unregistered securities without appropriate exemption 
(b) Investments from sanctioned entities 
(c) Ponzi or pyramid schemes 
(d) Assets violating client's investment policy 
[NOTE: Topic 18.2 content missing - numbering gap] 
18.3 Leveraged and Derivative Strategies 
Use of leverage or derivatives requires: 
(a) Client sophistication assessment 
(b) Written acknowledgment of risks 
(c) Enhanced monitoring per Topic 5.2 
(d) Compliance with Topic 18.2 restrictions 
**SEE Topic 18.2 for additional leverage limitations** 
18.4 Concentration Limits 
Unless specifically approved by client: 
(a) No single position exceeding 20% of portfolio 
(b) No sector exceeding 30% of portfolio 
(c) Maximum 10% allocation to illiquid investments 
(d) Zone-C asset limits per regional guidelines 
TOPIC 19: SANCTIONS AND PROHIBITED PERSONS 
19.1 Sanctions Screening 
Before accepting any client, screen against: 
(a) OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List 
(b) UN Sanctions Lists 
(c) Jurisdiction-specific sanctions 
(d) Internal prohibited persons list 
19.2 Ongoing Monitoring 
Sanctions screening must be performed: 
(a) At account opening 
(b) Monthly thereafter 
(c) Before executing any transaction 
(d) Upon notification of sanctions updates 
19.3 Blocked Assets 
If client or assets become sanctioned: 
(a) Immediately freeze account 
(b) Notify regulatory authority within 24 hours 
(c) Prohibit all transactions 
(d) File required reports per Topic 6.2 
TOPIC 20: TRANSACTION REPORTING AND RECORDKEEPING 
20.1 Transaction Reports 
For each transaction, maintain records of: 
(a) Date and time 
(b) Security identification 
(c) Quantity and price 
(d) Executing broker 
(e) Client authorization 
20.2 Communication Records 
Retain all communications including: 
(a) Emails related to advisory services 
(b) Instant messages 
(c) Text messages regarding client accounts 
(d) Voice recordings if maintained 
20.3 Retention Periods 
Records must be retained for: 
(a) Minimum 7 years for transaction records 
(b) Minimum 5 years for correspondence 
(c) Indefinitely for client agreements 
(d) Per Topic 30 for specialized records 
TOPIC 21: THIRD-PARTY VERIFICATION 
21.1 Performance Verification 
Performance claims must be verified by: 
(a) Independent third-party verifier 
(b) Annually at minimum 
(c) Using recognized calculation methodology (GIPS compliant) 
(d) Unless exempt per Topic 15.1 
21.2 Compliance Certification 
Annual compliance certification requires: 
(a) Review by external compliance consultant 
(b) Testing of key controls 
(c) Written certification report 
(d) Filing with regulatory authority 
21.3 Qualified Verifiers 
Third-party verifiers must: 
(a) Be independent of the Financial Institution 
(b) Have relevant professional credentials 
(c) Maintain professional liability insurance 
(d) Be approved by regulatory authority 
TOPIC 22: STANDARDS OF PROFESSIONAL CONDUCT 
22.1 Ethical Standards 
Qualified Advisors must: 
(a) Act with integrity and honesty 
(b) Maintain professional competence per Topic 8 
(c) Avoid conflicts of interest per Topic 14 
(d) Exercise fiduciary duties per Topic 3 
22.2 Prohibited Conduct 
Violations include: 
(a) Fraud or material misrepresentation 
(b) Theft or misappropriation 
(c) Insider trading 
(d) Market manipulation 
22.3 Disciplinary History Disclosure 
Must disclose on Form ADV: 
(a) Criminal convictions 
(b) Regulatory sanctions 
(c) Civil judgments related to advisory activities 
(d) Customer complaints resulting in settlements over $15,000 
TOPIC 23: BUSINESS CONTINUITY PLANNING 
23.1 Written Plan Required 
All Financial Institutions must maintain written business continuity plan addressing: 
(a) Data backup and recovery 
(b) Alternative work locations 
(c) Communication procedures 
(d) Critical vendor dependencies 
23.2 Testing Requirements 
Business continuity plans must be: 
(a) Tested annually 
(b) Updated based on test results 
(c) Communicated to all employees 
(d) Filed with regulatory authority upon request 
23.3 Disaster Recovery 
Recovery time objectives (RTO): 
(a) Critical systems: 4 hours 
(b) Client access to accounts: 24 hours 
(c) Full operational capacity: 72 hours 
TOPIC 24: ADVISORY AGREEMENTS 
24.1 Required Contract Terms 
All advisory agreements must include: 
(a) Scope of services 
(b) Fee structure and calculation method 
(c) Termination provisions 
(d) Assignment restrictions 
(e) Dispute resolution procedures 
24.2 Material Amendments 
Changes to advisory agreements require: 
(a) Written notice to client 
(b) 30 days advance notice 
(c) Client consent for material changes 
(d) Updated Form ADV delivery 
24.3 Termination 
Either party may terminate: 
(a) With or without cause 
(b) Upon 30 days written notice 
(c) Effective immediately for cause 
(d) With pro-rata fee refund 
TOPIC 25: ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTIES 
25.1 Violations 
Violations of this Framework may result in: 
(a) Warning letters 
(b) Monetary penalties up to $500,000 per violation 
(c) Suspension of registration 
(d) Permanent bar from industry 
25.2 Penalty Guidelines 
Factors considered in determining penalties: 
(a) Severity of violation 
(b) Harm to clients 
(c) Whether violation was intentional 
(d) Prior disciplinary history 
(e) Cooperation with investigation 
25.3 Appeals Process 
Respondents may appeal: 
(a) To Appeals Board within 30 days 
(b) Must file written notice of appeal 
(c) May request oral hearing 
(d) Decision final after appeal exhausted 
TOPIC 26: REPORTING TO REGULATORY AUTHORITIES 
26.1 Quarterly Reports 
Financial Institutions must file: 
(a) Form ADV updates within 90 days of fiscal year end 
(b) AUM reports 
(c) Disciplinary events within 30 days 
(d) Material business changes within 30 days 
26.2 Annual Filings 
Required annual submissions: 
(a) Audited financial statements (if applicable) 
(b) Compliance certification per Topic 21.2 
(c) Insurance certificates per Topic 12 
(d) Business continuity plan updates 
26.3 Event Reporting 
Immediate reporting required for: 
(a) Data breaches per Topic 16.2 
(b) Sanctions hits per Topic 19.3 
(c) Criminal investigations 
(d) Significant client complaints 
TOPIC 27: GRANDFATHERING AND TRANSITION PROVISIONS 
27.1 Existing Registrants 
Financial Institutions registered prior to January 1, 2096: 
(a) Have 180 days to achieve full compliance 
(b) Must file transition plan within 60 days 
(c) May request extension for good cause 
(d) Subject to enhanced monitoring during transition 
27.2 Legacy Client Accounts 
Accounts established before effective date: 
(a) May continue under prior fee arrangements for 12 months 
(b) Must comply with disclosure requirements immediately 
(c) Must transition to compliant agreements by December 31, 2096 
27.3 Restricted Jurisdiction Exception 
Benefit X is optional for Financial Institutions operating solely in non-participating 
jurisdictions, unless Zone-C operations require compliance. 
TOPIC 28: INCIDENT REPORTING 
28.1 Reportable Incidents 
Must report within specified timeframes: 
(a) Cybersecurity incidents per Topic 16.2 
(b) Suspected fraud or theft: immediately 
(c) Operational failures affecting >100 clients: 24 hours 
(d) Material compliance breaches: 48 hours 
28.2 Report Format 
All incident reports must include: 
(a) Description of incident 
(b) Date and time discovered 
(c) Affected clients or assets 
(d) Remedial actions taken 
(e) Preventive measures implemented 
28.3 Follow-up Reporting 
For significant incidents: 
(a) Preliminary report within initial timeframe 
(b) Detailed report within 30 days 
(c) Root cause analysis within 60 days 
(d) Corrective action plan within 90 days 
TOPIC 29: SUPERVISION AND OVERSIGHT 
29.1 Supervisory Structure 
Financial Institutions must establish: 
(a) Clear reporting hierarchy 
(b) Designated supervisors for each advisor 
(c) Written supervisory procedures 
(d) Regular supervision reviews 
29.2 Supervisor Qualifications 
Supervisors must: 
(a) Be Qualified Advisors per Topic 8 
(b) Have minimum 5 years industry experience 
(c) Complete supervisor training 
(d) Maintain clean disciplinary record 
29.3 Supervisory Reviews 
Must conduct: 
(a) Real-time transaction monitoring 
(b) Quarterly account reviews 
(c) Annual performance evaluations 
(d) Documentation of all reviews 
TOPIC 30: ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE FOR HIGH-RISK CLIENTS 
30.1 Risk Assessment 
Enhanced due diligence required for clients who: 
(a) Are Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) 
(b) Have source of wealth from high-risk jurisdictions 
(c) Engage in complex ownership structures 
(d) Maintain substantial asset holdings in Zone-C 
30.2 Documentation Requirements 
For high-risk clients, maintain: 
(a) Source of wealth verification 
(b) Ongoing transaction monitoring 
(c) Annual re-verification of identity 
(d) Enhanced recordkeeping per Topic 20.3 
30.3 Approval Requirements 
Acceptance of high-risk clients requires: 
(a) Senior management approval 
(b) Documented risk assessment 
(c) Enhanced monitoring plan 
(d) Compliance officer sign-off 
TOPIC 31: BENEFICIAL OWNERSHIP TRANSPARENCY 
31.1 Disclosure Requirements 
Financial Institutions must identify and verify: 
(a) All natural persons with 25% or greater ownership 
(b) Persons exercising control regardless of ownership percentage 
(c) Ultimate beneficial owners through all entity layers 
(d) Changes in beneficial ownership within 30 days 
31.2 Documentation Standards 
For each beneficial owner, obtain: 
(a) Full legal name 
(b) Date of birth 
(c) Residential address 
(d) Government identification number 
(e) Ownership percentage 
31.3 Corporate Clients 
For corporate clients: 
(a) Obtain certified ownership structure chart 
(b) Identify beneficial owners per 31.1 
(c) Verify through independent sources 
(d) Update annually or upon material change 
31.4 Legal Entity Ownership Disclosure 
When beneficial owners are legal entities (not natural persons): 
(a) Must continue disclosure through all entity layers 
(b) Document corporate structure at each level 
(c) Identify natural persons at ultimate beneficial owner level 
(d) Certification from client required 
**EXAMPLE DEFICIENCY: Topic 31 of GFSAF requires investment advisers to disclose all 
beneficial owners with 25% or greater ownership interest. Your Form ADV Part 1A, 
Schedule A listed XYZ Holdings LLC as a 25% owner but didn't identify the natural 
persons with ownership/control of XYZ Holdings. Per Topic 31.4, ownership disclosure 
must continue through all entity layers until natural persons are identified. To cure this deficiency, file an amended Form ADV within 30 days listing the ultimate beneficial owners of XYZ Holdings LLC with supporting documentation.** 
TOPIC 32: COMPLAINT HANDLING AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION 
32.1 Complaint Procedures 
Financial Institutions must: 
(a) Maintain written complaint procedures 
(b) Acknowledge complaints within 5 business days 
(c) Provide substantive response within 30 days 
(d) Maintain complaint log 
32.2 Mandatory Arbitration 
Advisory agreements may include arbitration clauses, provided: 
(a) Clause is conspicuous 
(b) Client initials arbitration provision 
(c) Choice of arbitration forum disclosed 
(d) Arbitration procedures explained 
32.3 Regulatory Reporting 
Must report to regulatory authority: 
(a) Complaints alleging fraud 
(b) Complaints seeking damages >$50,000 
(c) Complaints resulting in settlements >$15,000 
(d) Patterns of similar complaints

APPENDIX B: DEFINED TERMS 
"Assets Under Management (AUM)": Total market value of client assets over which Financial Institution exercises discretionary investment authority. 
"Beneficial Owner": Any natural person who directly or indirectly owns or controls 25% 
or more of equity interests, or exercises control over the entity. 
"High-Risk Investment": Securities with volatility exceeding 25% annually, leverage ratios above 2:1, or illiquidity periods exceeding 90 days. 
"Material Conflict": Any conflict reasonably expected to influence advisor's judgment to 
detriment of client. 
"Qualified Client": Client meeting criteria in Topic 13.1 for performance-based fee 
eligibility. 
"Zone-C": [UNDEFINED - Referenced throughout document but never explicitly defined] 
"Zone-C Advisors": Financial advisors maintaining substantial operations in Zone-C. 
(Zone-C itself not defined)
